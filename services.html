The Formal Route develops tools and methods for designing and digitising signalling projects, their modelling, and formal, fully automated verification of their safety.
We use the state of the art verification techniques (automated theorem provers and solvers) to ensure fully-automated scalable verification of signalling designs.

We offer support for the control table designs and for digitising of the existing railway layouts. This is accompanied by the comprehensive verification of the digital assets.
Our main differentiator between the existing analysis and verification tools and our approach is that our methods and tools fully hide the formal methods and use exclusively the notations deployed by the railway industry.

Digital signalling plan
We are able to capture signalling plans in a digital, conceptual, graph-base format with export to formats such as railML and SDEF.
Formal verification of railway interlocking
We have an established expertise in automated formal verification of SSI-based interlocking data to meet and exceed requirements of NR/L2/SIG/11201/Mod B11. Our method and toolset are approved and recommended by SSI WG and Network Rail.
Automated layout recognition
Using state-of-the-art machine learning technique we can automatically transform a CAD drawing of signalling plan into a conceptual model; we also read and interpret route tables
